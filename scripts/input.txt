Control your mornings.

The messages.

Because here is what nobody tells you about mornings.

Rule three.
Plan every purchase in advance.
Most financial mistakes share one common cause.
Impulse.
You see something.
You want it.
You buy it.
Later you regret it.
Here is the system that eliminates that cycle entirely.
Whenever you feel the urge to buy something write it down instead.
Do not buy it.
Write it down.
Then wait.
One week minimum.
One month for anything over one hundred dollars.
Let the initial excitement fade.
Because here is what you will discover.
Most desires die with time.
The thing you absolutely needed on Tuesday becomes something you cannot even remember wanting by the following Tuesday.
This one rule alone can save you thousands annually.
Because most purchases you think you need today you will not remember next month.
The minimalist does not buy to feel good in the moment.
They buy to solve real problems or create long term value.
And that distinction is everything.

No clear picture of what comes in or goes out.

Here is the system that actually works.
One checking account.
One savings account.
One credit card paid in full every month.
Automated bills.
Automated savings.
That is it.
Simplicity brings clarity.
Clarity brings control.
And control is where wealth begins.
I have seen people with six bank accounts and seven credit cards who have absolutely no idea where their money goes.

Rule five.
Set three annual priorities maximum.
Not ten.
Not seven.
Three.
Here is why this matters more than most people realize.
Without focus life scatters.
The year passes and you look back wondering where it went.
Set no more than three annual priorities.
Clear goals that if achieved transform your life.
Save fifteen thousand dollars.
Pay off credit card debt.
Build a specific skill.
Every time you are tempted to chase a new goal or opportunity ask yourself one question.
Does this serve one of my three priorities.
If the answer is no it is a distraction.
Cut it immediately.
Minimalists know that trying to do everything leads to nothing.
Doing a few things with complete focus leads to wealth.

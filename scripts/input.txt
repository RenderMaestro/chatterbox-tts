Rule one.
Eliminate toxic debt immediately.
Not eventually.
Not after the holidays.
Now.
Here is why this is rule one and not rule ten.
Some debt builds wealth.
A mortgage that lets you own appreciating property.
A business loan that generates income.
But most debt is poison.
Credit cards at twenty five percent.
Payday loans.
Financing furniture you do not need.
Here is the math nobody shows you.
If you are paying twenty percent interest while earning ten percent on investments you are losing ten percent annually.
Every single year.
Guaranteed.
That is not a wealth building strategy.
That is a wealth destruction strategy dressed up as normal life.

Rule three.
Plan every purchase in advance.

You buy it.

Here is the system that eliminates that cycle entirely.
Whenever you feel the urge to buy something write it down instead.

One month for anything over one hundred dollars.
Let the initial excitement fade.
Because here is what you will discover.
Most desires die with time.
The thing you absolutely needed on Tuesday becomes something you cannot even remember wanting by the following Tuesday.
This one rule alone can save you thousands annually.
Because most purchases you think you need today you will not remember next month.

They buy to solve real problems or create long term value.

Rule ten.
Create a thirty day waiting list for desires.
Not every desire deserves immediate action.
Most are just sparks that vanish quickly.
Here is the system.
When you want something write it down.
Date it.
And wait thirty days before making any decision.
After thirty days one of two things has happened.
Either the desire is still present and it may genuinely be worth considering.
Or it has completely disappeared and you have saved yourself from another unnecessary expense.
Amazon wish lists work perfectly for this.
Add items.
Check back monthly.
You will be shocked how many things you wanted desperately that you cannot even remember adding.
This rule builds discipline clarity and genuine respect for your money.
And it compounds.
Because every month you run this system you save more than the month before.

Quality wins on pure mathematics.
Every time.
Without exception.
Apply this thinking to everything.
Fewer clothes but durable ones.
Fewer gadgets but ones that last.
Fewer relationships but meaningful ones.
Fewer commitments but significant ones.
When you invest in quality you waste less.
And wealth follows naturally from wasting less.
